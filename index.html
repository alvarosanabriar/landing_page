When I evoked the memory of my grand aunt, her eyes always seemed to hold a certain sadness—a pain she chose to bear in silence. Yet in this photograph she kept hidden, a relic of defiance, she appeared transformed. She looked joyful, daring, liberated. Did this act of rebellion condemned her to a life of ostracism and oblivion?
